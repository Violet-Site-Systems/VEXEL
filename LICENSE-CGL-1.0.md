### 2. Governance Principles

Projects using this license must adhere to:

a) **Democratic Decision-Making**: Establish transparent processes for community participation in significant project decisions.

b) **Inclusive Leadership**: Ensure leadership structures are accessible and representative of the community's diversity.

c) **Transparency**: Maintain open communication channels and publicly document major decisions and their rationale.

d) **Accountability**: Create mechanisms for holding project leaders and contributors accountable to the community.

### 3. Community Rights

The community has the right to:

a) **Participation**: Meaningfully participate in project governance and decision-making processes.

b) **Information Access**: Access project information, roadmaps, and decision-making processes.

c) **Voice and Feedback**: Provide input on project direction and have that input genuinely considered.

d) **Fair Treatment**: Be treated equitably regardless of contribution level, background, or identity.

### 4. Governance Structure Requirements

Projects must:

a) **Document Governance**: Maintain clear, public documentation of governance structures and processes.

b) **Code of Conduct**: Establish and enforce a code of conduct that promotes respectful, inclusive participation.

c) **Dispute Resolution**: Implement fair processes for resolving conflicts and disputes.

d) **Regular Elections**: If using elected leadership, conduct regular, fair elections with term limits.

e) **Contribution Recognition**: Acknowledge and value diverse forms of contribution beyond code.

### 5. Power Distribution

a) **Checks and Balances**: No single entity or individual should have unilateral control over critical project decisions.

b) **Distributed Authority**: Delegate authority across multiple roles and individuals.

c) **Community Veto**: Provide mechanisms for the community to challenge decisions that violate project values.

### 6. Stakeholder Inclusion

Governance must consider:

- End users and their needs
- Contributors of all types (code, documentation, community support, etc.)
- Organizations and entities that depend on the software
- Affected communities and populations

### 7. Adaptation and Evolution

Governance structures should:

- Evolve based on community needs and feedback
- Be reviewed and updated regularly
- Allow for community-initiated improvements

### 8. Prohibited Governance Practices

Projects may not:

- Silence or exclude community members arbitrarily
- Make critical decisions without community input
- Use governance structures to entrench power
- Discriminate in participation or leadership opportunities
